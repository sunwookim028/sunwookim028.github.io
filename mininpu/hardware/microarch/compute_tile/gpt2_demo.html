    GPT-2 demo: the forward pass as tile kernels¶

    The GPT-2 (124M) forward pass is the v1 worked example that exercises the compute tile: it lowers to the v1 opcode set and runs as a sequence of per-kernel schedules on one tile. GPT-2 is the demo, not the architecture's identity (the tile is a composable IP template). This page is a skeleton: the kernel decomposition and the per-kernel Gantt schedules are generated from the lowering and cost model (see Sources); roll-up cycle numbers are not authored here while the GELU decomposition is still open (#385).

    The GPT-2 forward as tile kernels¶

    The 124M forward pass lowers to the v1 opcodes and runs as per-kernel schedules on one compute tile. Each transformer block is a fixed sequence of MXU-bound and VPU-bound kernels over SPAD / VREG / ACC, with the DMU staging weights and activations from device memory.

    Kernel decomposition of a transformer block¶

    A transformer block decomposes into these operator → kernel groups (the full end-to-end pass adds the embedding at the front and the LM-head tail at the end):

    • embedding
    • layernorm (pre-attention and pre-MLP)
    • attention: qkv projection · qk scores · softmax · av · out_proj
    • residual add
    • mlp: fc · gelu · proj
    • ln_f + lm_head
    • argmax

    The authoritative operator → opcode lowering is dv/workload/gpt2_lowering.py.

    Reading a kernel Gantt¶

    Each generated kernel schedule is a Gantt over 7 fixed lanes --- SEQ, wbufA, wbufB, ACC, LSU, VPU, DMU --- against a cycle time axis, with a shared legend. The lanes show which unit is busy each cycle and where the overlap levers (weight double-buffer, async-DMA ping-pong) hide latency. The lane / overlap idiom is shared with the compute-tile ct-pipeline-overlap figure.

    MXU-bound kernels¶

    The matmul-heavy kernels --- qkv_proj, qk_scores, cproj --- are MXU-bound: they stream weight and X tiles through the systolic array and accumulate in ACC.

    VPU-bound kernels¶

    The normalization and activation kernels --- softmax, layernorm, gelu --- are VPU-bound: reductions, transcendental LUTs, and per-lane arithmetic.

    gpt2_gelu is HELD pending the GELU decomposition (#385): its single-fused-op schedule is known-wrong until the op is decomposed, so no GELU roll-up is authored here.

    Residual and LM-head tail¶

    The block tail adds the residual, and the pass ends with ln_f, the lm_head projection, argmax, and the flush / halt drain.

    Sources¶

    • dv/workload/gpt2_lowering.py --- the operator → opcode lowering.
    • mininpu/cost/programs_gpt2.py --- the per-kernel cost / schedule programs the Gantts are generated from.
