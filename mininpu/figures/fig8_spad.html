Δ notes vs current RTL

  • DMU is a black box on Port A. The locked SPAD spec fixes only the port: a 256b DM bus (32 B/cy internal) into Port A, backed by an off-tile DRAM HP port at 4 B/cy (8× narrower). DMU internals — descriptor slots, AGU, burst/DMA engine — are deferred and deliberately not drawn.
  • Port B is lightened. Under the VPU = VREG-only spine, Port B's two arbitrated compute masters are just mxu.load.w (bf16 weight tiles) and the VPU vreg.load / vreg.store cast traffic (SPAD↔VREG, bf16↔fp32). The old ACC-row-stream traffic on the current 2-compute-master ACC-row bus is gone — Port B is materially lighter.
  • 8 banks, bank = addr%8. 1024 words / 8 = 128 words/bank. Port B grants 1 master / cycle (MXU or VPU); the 8-bank layout limits conflicts within a master's access, keeping arbitration cheap.
  • Packing. A 256b word holds 2 bf16 tile-rows (each tile-row = 8×bf16 = 128b); SPAD stores bf16 weights, bf16 activations (X tiles), bias rows, and kernel outputs (Y).
